Garage Heat & Show Plan (RTX 5070 + Ryzen 9 7950X)
=================================================

Objective
---------
Run a living, ever-evolving bio simulation at 1920x1080 that maxes GPU + CPU, turning the research garage into a controllable heat chamber while projecting mesmerizing emergent lifeforms.

Strategy Summary
----------------
1. **GPU-bound Gray-Scott / Lenia Hybrid**
	- Ping-pong framebuffers at 1920x1080 with multiple simulation substeps per frame.
	- Heavy kernel convolutions + nonlinear reaction-diffusion with compute-like fragment shaders.
	- Parameter drift + color LUTs for "living lab" aesthetics.

2. **CPU Saturation Threads**
	- Background NumPy-heavy matrix multiplies and FFTs per core.
	- Adjustable worker count up to 32 threads to max out Ryzen 9 7950X.

3. **Thermal Safety & Practicalities**
	- Enforce VSYNC-off, unlimited FPS.
	- Provide monitoring tips (HWInfo64, MSI Afterburner) and thermal throttling safeguards.
	- Offer start/stop instructions and optional "eco" parameters for bench testing.

Artifacts to Produce
--------------------
- `main.py`: ModernGL window config + CPU burner threads.
- `shaders/life_update.frag` & `shaders/life_display.frag`: Reaction-diffusion core + display pass.
- `requirements.txt`: Python deps (moderngl, moderngl-window, numpy, pyrr).
- `README.md`: Setup, tuning, safety checklist, and show choreography ideas.

Next Steps
----------
1. Scaffold repo structure and shader assets.
2. Implement configurable CLI parameters (resolution, iterations, exposure, CPU workers).
3. Smoke-test at reduced resolution (e.g., 1920x1080) to validate pipeline.
4. Document run-book for 1080p life lab deployment (projecting onto door panel).
